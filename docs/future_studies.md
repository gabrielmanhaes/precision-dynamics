# Proposed Future Studies

Studies that would validate or disconfirm the framework's key claims,
ordered by tractability.

---

## Study 1: HRV treatment selection (HIGH PRIORITY — existing data)

**Prediction:** High-HRV depression -> better psilocybin response.
Low-HRV depression -> better SSRI response.

**Method:** Secondary analysis of existing clinical trial datasets.
- COMPASS psilocybin trial (N=233): contact Goodwin et al. for HRV data
- STAR*D (N~4000, publicly available via NIMH): check for ECG measures
- Imperial psilocybin vs escitalopram (N=59): contact Carhart-Harris lab

**What to measure:** Baseline RMSSD before treatment. Remission at
6 weeks. Compare remission rates by HRV tertile within each arm.

**Preregistration:** Required before analysis. Specific criteria:
>=10% differential remission rate in predicted directions, median HRV split.

**Timeline:** 3-6 months if data access granted.

## Study 2: SD(LZW) as chronicity predictor (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Prediction:** SD(LZW) across time lower in depressed patients than
controls. Predicts treatment response better than mean LZW. Restored
by successful treatment.

**Method:** Ambulatory EEG in depressed patients vs controls, 72-hour
recording. Compute LZW complexity variability. Correlate with treatment
response at 8 weeks.

**Timeline:** 18-24 months (requires new data collection).

---

## Study 3: Absorption-meditation-dreaming-psychedelics factor analysis (MEDIUM PRIORITY — tractable survey study)

**Prediction:** Meditation depth, lucid dreaming frequency, psychedelic
response depth, hypnagogic creativity, and absorption (Tellegen) load
on a single latent factor reflecting P_selfmodel reduction capacity.

**Method:** Online survey, N>=300. Measures:
- Tellegen Absorption Scale (validated)
- Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index + dream recall frequency
- Lucid dreaming frequency and depth (LuCiD scale)
- Mystical Experience Questionnaire (for participants with prior
  psychedelic experience — optional module)
- Brief meditation experience and depth rating
- Novel hypnagogic creativity measure (to be developed)

Factor analysis. If single-factor solution fits, validates unification.

**Timeline:** 6-12 months.

---

## Study 4: Convergent validity of HRV as precision proxy (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Prediction:** HRV, pupil dilation variability, EEG alpha variability,
and P300 amplitude all load on a single latent precision factor.

**Method:** N>=60 depressed patients + N>=60 controls. Measure all four
in the same session under standardized conditions. Factor analysis.

**Timeline:** 18-24 months.

---

## Study 5: Propofol vs psilocybin post-acute plasticity comparison (LOWER PRIORITY — complex design)

**Prediction:** Propofol and high-dose psilocybin produce equivalent
P_selfmodel reduction (similar DMN coherence loss, similar subjective
"loss of consciousness") but ONLY psilocybin produces post-acute TEMP
(plasticity window, personality change, therapeutic effect).

**Method:** Neuroimaging study measuring DMN coherence and plasticity
markers (BDNF, synaptic density via SV2A PET) at baseline, during, and
48h after propofol vs psilocybin in healthy volunteers. Depression
symptom measures in patient population.

**Timeline:** 3-5 years (requires new drug trial infrastructure).

---

## Study 6: REM disruption and canalization progression (LOWER PRIORITY — longitudinal)

**Prediction:** Experimental REM suppression over 4 weeks increases
attractor depth (measured by LZW variability reduction and HRV decrease)
and reduces responsiveness to subsequent psilocybin administration.

**Method:** Crossover design, healthy volunteers. Phase 1: baseline
measures. Phase 2: 4 weeks REM suppression via REM-suppressing agent.
Phase 3: psilocybin session with mystical experience measure. Compare
to control arm (no REM suppression).

**Ethical consideration:** REM suppression for 4 weeks may have adverse
effects — requires careful IRB review and monitoring.

**Timeline:** 2-3 years.
